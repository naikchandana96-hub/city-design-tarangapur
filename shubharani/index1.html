1. Multilingual BPO & IT Services Hub

Round-Robin Scheduling SDG 8
Fairly distributes tasks/calls among agents to ensure equitable workload, prevent burnout, and maximize service availability.

2. Smart Port Security & Resource Allocation

Dijkstra + Stable Matching SDG 9
Uses Dijkstra's to find the quickest path to an incident, then Stable Matching to assign the best-suited unit based on skills/availability, ensuring a reliable, fast response.

3. Port-Based Ship Repair & Maintenance Hub

Assembly-Line Scheduling (DP) SDG 9
Dynamic Programming is used to schedule multi-stage ship repair across parallel repair lines (workstations), minimizing total completion time (makespan).

4. Harbor Logistics Truck Processing

FIFO + Greedy + Simulation SDG 12
Manages truck queues (FIFO) but uses a Greedy (Shortest Job First) approach at processing bottlenecks (scanners) to prioritize fast tasks, minimizing idling and congestion.

5. Port Infrastructure & Cold Storage Optimization

TSP (DP + Heuristics) SDG 13
Models cargo delivery as a Traveling Salesperson Problem (TSP). Uses Dynamic Programming and Greedy heuristics (like Clark-Wright) to find fuel-saving, minimal-distance routes.

6. Beach Wedding & Events Hub Scheduling

Greedy Interval Scheduling SDG 8
Sorts events by earliest finish time and greedily selects non-overlapping events to maximize venue utilization and total scheduled activity.

7. EV Bike & Water Sports Rental Management

Hash Tables + Min-Heap Priority Queue SDG 12
Hash Tables enable instant asset lookup. A Min-Heap Priority Queue prioritizes maintenance/charging tasks based on lowest battery level, maximizing asset lifespan and readiness.

8. Coastal Food Truck Fleet Logistics & Inventory

VRP-C (Clark-Wright Savings) SDG 13
Solves the Vehicle Routing Problem with Capacity Constraints (VRP-C) using the Clark-Wright heuristic to merge delivery routes, minimizing fleet mileage and carbon emissions.

üè¶ 3. Public Services & Customer Management (SDG 8, 9, 16)

9. Public Service Queue Management

Multi-Level Priority Queues SDG 16
Uses a Max-Heap for emergencies (highest priority) and a FIFO/Round-Robin queue for standard requests, ensuring both fairness and urgency in service delivery.

10. Fast Facility Lookup & Navigation System

Trie (Prefix Tree) SDG 9
The Trie data structure enables $O(L)$ time complexity for prefix-based searching and autocomplete in facility navigation, where $L$ is the length of the query.

11. Digital Advertising & Events Hub Scheduling

Greedy Interval Scheduling SDG 8
Schedules advertising slots or events based on earliest finish time to maximize the total number of non-overlapping activities and revenue generated from the digital platform.

12. Spa & Appointment Booking System

Segment Trees (Interval Queries) SDG 3
Segment Trees handle range updates (booking a slot) and range queries (checking availability over an interval) in $O(\log N)$ time, ensuring instant scheduling responses.

13. Mental Health & Rehabilitation Hub Grouping

K-Means Clustering SDG 3
Uses K-Means Clustering (unsupervised learning) to mathematically group patients into optimal, compatible therapy groups based on their symptom profile and needs.

14. Marine Equipment Manufacturing Unit Sequencing

Greedy Job Sequencing with Deadlines SDG 9
Sorts manufacturing jobs by profit in descending order and schedules them in the latest possible time slot before their deadline to maximize total profit from production.
